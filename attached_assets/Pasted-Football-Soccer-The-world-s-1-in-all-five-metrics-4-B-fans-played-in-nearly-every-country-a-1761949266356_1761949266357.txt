Football (Soccer) — The world’s #1 in all five metrics. 4 B+ fans, played in nearly every country, and dominates global betting volume (~50%+).

Basketball — Huge in the U.S., China, Europe, and the Philippines. Massive global participation and betting interest, especially NBA.

Tennis — One of the top three in betting turnover globally; elite tours are year-round and worldwide.

Cricket — Enormous in India, Pakistan, England, and Australia; high betting volumes in South Asia.

American Football (Gridiron) — NFL drives immense viewership and betting in North America; limited participation globally but massive revenues.

Horse Racing — Historically huge in betting (especially UK, Japan, Hong Kong, Australia). Viewership is niche but betting volume remains enormous.

Esports — Rapid growth; huge participation, young audience, global reach, rising betting activity across games like CS2, LoL, and Dota 2.

Motor Racing (Formula 1, MotoGP, NASCAR) — Massive viewership and sponsorship globally; mid-tier betting but enormous fan base.

Baseball — Huge in the U.S., Japan, Korea, and Latin America; moderate global betting but strong participation.

Golf — Wealthy demographics, steady betting activity, and worldwide professional tours.

⚡ Second Tier (Strong Regional Powerhouses)

Boxing — Huge betting draw for major fights; global fan base, though less participation.

Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) — UFC drives high betting volume and global visibility; limited amateur participation.

Ice Hockey — Very strong in North America, Northern Europe, and Russia; steady betting scene in Canada and Scandinavia.

Rugby Union — Popular in UK, France, Australia, NZ, and South Africa; moderate betting.

Rugby League — Strong in Australia and Northern England; smaller global spread but heavy betting locally.

Table Tennis — Extremely high participation in Asia and Europe; lower betting volume globally.

Badminton — Huge participation in Asia (especially China, Indonesia, India); low betting but vast player base.

Volleyball — One of the most-played sports globally; moderate viewership, low betting turnover.

Cycling — Strong European following, major events (Tour de France) draw large viewership; niche betting.

Snooker / Pool / Billiards — Popular in UK and China; moderate betting scene.

⚾ Third Tier (Popular but Niche in Betting)

Handball — Strong in Europe, limited globally; some betting interest during championships.

Darts — Surprisingly high betting volume in the UK; modest global reach.

Athletics / Track & Field — Enormous Olympic viewership but low betting except major events.

Wrestling (Pro/Entertainment) — Huge entertainment viewership (WWE), low legitimate betting.

Surfing — High lifestyle participation, low betting, global coastal audience.

Motocross / Supercross — Strong in U.S. and parts of Europe; small betting ecosystem.

Skiing / Snowboarding — Big in alpine countries, niche elsewhere.

Gymnastics — Large Olympic viewership, low participation outside youth programs.

Field Hockey — Big in India, the Netherlands, and Australia; limited betting.

Lacrosse — Growing in North America; minimal global spread or betting.

🏅 Lower Tier (Niche or Regional Sports)

Netball — Popular in Australia, NZ, and parts of the UK; little betting.

Bowling — Recreationally huge, but not a betting or spectator sport.

Softball — Primarily U.S./Japan; limited global audience.

Cricket (Indoor / Variants) — Exists mainly in Commonwealth nations; tiny betting share.

Gaelic Sports (Hurling, Gaelic Football) — Popular only in Ireland; passionate local following.

Aussie Rules Football — Enormous in Australia, limited international reach but high local betting.

Curling — Niche outside Winter Olympics; small audience and betting base.
